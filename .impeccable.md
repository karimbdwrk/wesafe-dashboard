## Design Context

### Users

Three distinct audiences, all using the same platform:

1. **Job seekers** — Agents de sécurité in France browsing and applying for missions and permanent positions. Many work nights. Mobile is their primary device (iOS/Android apps). They apply for everything from standard guard posts to premium events (Fashion Week, luxury retail, galas). They need to act fast — last-minute missions are time-sensitive.

2. **Employers** — Security companies and agencies posting jobs, reviewing candidates, and generating contracts. They live in the dashboard all day. Their job is to fill positions quickly, reduce paperwork, and manage a roster. Time and money are their primary concerns.

3. **Internal admins** — Platform team managing users, content, moderation, and platform operations. They need density and control.

**Shared job to be done**: Get the right person in the right place, with the least friction. Every feature should save time, reduce manual work, and eliminate the "tâches chiantes" (tedious admin tasks).

### Brand Personality

**3-word voice**: precise, authoritative, efficient

WeSafe is not a generic job board — it is a specialized command center for security staffing. It serves premium clients including luxury fashion events (Fashion Week, luxury retail security). The brand must feel upmarket and serious, not budget-tier. Think less "job board," more "operations platform."

**Emotional goal**: Users should feel they are using a premium enterprise tool that respects their intelligence and their time. Confident and in control — like a professional instrument, not a consumer app.

**What it must NOT feel like**:
- securityjob.fr — generic, low-effort, bargain-bin aesthetics
- jobsinparis.fr — undifferentiated job board
- Consumer-grade HR tools (Pôle Emploi, Indeed) — too casual, no authority

### Aesthetic Direction

**Reference**: GitHub Advanced Security (https://github.com/security/advanced-security) — structured, enterprise-grade, high contrast, information-dense, restrained. No decoration that doesn't serve function.

**Visual tone**: Precision instrument meets premium enterprise software. Think operations control room. Every pixel intentional.

**Theme**: Dark as default. Security professionals work nights; the platform's operator context suits dark. Light mode available via toggle — must be equally polished, not an afterthought.

**Color direction**:
- Deep, near-black backgrounds with warm-tinted neutrals (not cold blue-gray, not pure black)
- High-contrast foreground text
- A single restrained accent — NOT cyan-on-dark (AI cliché). Consider warm slate, or a desaturated amber/gold that signals prestige without being gaudy
- NO: purple-to-blue gradients, neon glows, glassmorphism, gradient text

**Typography direction**:
- A confident, geometric or humanist sans-serif that reads as authoritative at small sizes
- NOT Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, Space Grotesk, or any other reflex-list font
- Strong hierarchy: clear size contrast between headings, labels, and body (at least 1.25× ratio between steps)

**Layout tone**:
- Information-dense but not cluttered
- Left-aligned, structured grids with intentional whitespace
- No identical card grids — vary composition
- No hero metric layouts (big number + small label + gradient accent)

### Design Principles

1. **Precision over decoration** — Every element earns its place. Decorative elements are banned unless they reinforce brand meaning. No border-left accents, no sparklines, no generic drop shadows.

2. **Efficiency is the feature** — The interface exists to save time and money. Fewer clicks to every key action. Dense layouts are acceptable when they serve the user — clutter is not the same as density.

3. **Premium through restraint** — High-end feel comes from execution quality: tight typography, intentional spacing, confident color use. Not from gradients or ornamentation.

4. **Trust at first glance** — Layout and typography must communicate reliability. This is a platform for professionals managing real contracts and real people.

5. **Dark-first, light-equal** — Dark mode is the default and the primary design surface. Light mode must be designed with equal care, not treated as an inversion.
